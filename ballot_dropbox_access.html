Project 5 – Ballot Dropbox Access Analysis

Course: GEOG 360 – GIS & Mapping | Date: Dec 2025

Tools: QGIS, Census Data, Voting Data, Shapefiles

Project Overview

This project analyzes the accessibility of ballot dropboxes across King County, Washington. The goal is to identify areas where residents, especially minority populations, may have limited access to dropboxes and to visualize spatial disparities in voting accessibility.

Data & Methodology

  • Data Sources: Census Tracts, Demographics Table, Voting Districts, Ballot Dropbox Locations, City & County Council Districts.
  • Processing Steps: Joined Demographics table to Census Tracts, created buffers (1–5 miles) around dropboxes, performed intersect with Voting Districts and Seattle City Council Districts.
  • Spatial Analysis: Attribute queries for voter registration, buffer analysis to show access distances, dot density mapping for minority voters, intersection analysis for voting districts.
  • Visualization Choices: Concentric buffers (1–5 miles), color-coded population density, map legends, and captions for clarity.

Research Question

How does racial composition influence the accessibility of ballot dropboxes in King County?

Key Insights

  • Approximately 44% of all voters in King County live within 1 mile of a ballot dropbox.
  • Some census tracts with high minority populations have lower access to dropboxes, highlighting potential disparities.
  • Spatial distribution of dropboxes is concentrated in urban areas, leaving rural or suburban tracts with reduced access.

Reflection & Skills Demonstrated

This project demonstrates spatial data management, attribute and spatial queries, buffer analysis, and visual storytelling in QGIS. Key skills include data cleaning, table joins, demographic analysis, and creating dot density maps for population estimation.
